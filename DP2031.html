Tailteann Sports Centre, Mary Immaculate College, Limerick.
2007, Murray Ã“ Laoire Architects and Chris Ralphs.
Study by Sam Mullan Galvin and James Pearce.

This dynamic bulding straddles and interacts with a recognized desire line from student accomodation to the main Mary I campus, and formed the first part of the campus-wide redevelopment.

Creating an architectural event along this route, the elevated plaza also forms a seamless entrance to the upper floor, overlooking both the indoor sports hall and the football pitch.

The roof spans 45 degrees and 45 metres across the building, an unusual approach, but a sensitive response to the need for low glare north lighting. This main volume is 9.1 metres tall, to allow for 6 world-standard badminton courts. The roof profile also allows stack effect ventilation through louvres at the extreme end of each bank of semi-translucent windows.
